# Alto Visual References

This folder holds local visual reference material for:

- Alto's Adventure
- Alto's Odyssey

The intent is to have a stable local reference pack we can reuse later when exploring visual directions for Gradient.

## Source Notes

The curated folders were assembled from official or near-official materials:

- Alto's Adventure original press kit screenshots
- Alto's Adventure: The Spirit of the Mountain press screenshots
- Alto's Odyssey original press kit screenshots
- Alto's Odyssey: The Lost City press screenshots
- official Apple App Store screenshots pulled from Apple's public listing API
- representative still frames extracted from official Odyssey press GIFs
- Fandom image pages for character-selection and workshop screens
- a review image used as an Odyssey title-screen fallback
- your own in-game screenshots from `Photos-3-001 (1).zip`

## Folder Layout

- `curated/screenshots`
  The main still-image set to browse first.
- `curated/ui_menus`
  Title screens, character selection, workshop, and other UI-heavy references.
- `curated/motion`
  Official gameplay GIFs that are useful for movement, timing, and feel.
- `curated/extras`
  Overlapping or lower-priority images that were kept out of the main set.
- `_source_zips`
  Downloaded press archives.
- `_source_store`
  Store screenshots pulled directly from Apple.
- `user_captures/raw`
  Your original screenshot dump, extracted locally from the ZIP you provided.
- `user_captures/contact_sheets`
  Fast overview sheets made from your own screenshots.
- other extracted folders
  Raw press-kit contents, including non-screenshot assets such as logos, promo art, concept art, and device shots.

## Notes On Menus

- Alto's Adventure now includes your own title, home, settings, and players screens in `curated/ui_menus`.
- Alto's Odyssey now includes your own title, home, settings, goals, players, and workshop screens in `curated/ui_menus`.
- The user-captured menu shots are now the strongest source for overall UI/theme direction.
- Earlier external menu references are still kept because they add a little extra coverage, but your captures should be treated as the primary menu reference set.
